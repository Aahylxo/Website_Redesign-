UAE E-Invoicing Rollout

The phased mandate, in plain English.

The rollout — set by Ministerial Decision 244/2025 and Cabinet Decision 106/2025 — opens with voluntary onboarding in July 2026, becomes mandatory for large taxpayers in January 2027, and reaches the rest of the economy by July 2027.

VOLUNTARY 1 July 2026

Voluntary go-live opens

Any UAE business

Onboard early without penalty, on your own schedule.

Start issuing structured invoices through your accredited ASP. Early movers get priority onboarding support and time to validate ERP data before the compliance rush.

PHASE 1 1 January 2027

Large taxpayers — mandatory

Annual revenue ≥ AED 50M

Clearance becomes mandatory for the largest taxpayers.

ASP must be appointed by 31 July 2026. Every invoice reported through DCTCE within 14 days of issuance.

PHASE 2 1 July 2027

All other VAT-registered

Revenue < AED 50M

The mandate reaches the majority of UAE businesses.

ASP must be appointed by 31 March 2027. Roughly 82% of UAE businesses fall in this band.

PHASE 3 1 October 2027

Government entities (B2G)

UAE government bodies

B2G invoicing comes into scope; B2C stays exempt.

ASP must be appointed by 31 March 2027. B2C transactions remain out of scope for the foreseeable future.

CABINET DECISION 106/2025 Penalties for non-compliance
AED 5,000/month — failure to implement
AED 100/invoice in wrong format
AED 1,000/day — system failure unreported
